Development timeline

Archway Campus

The short version of what happened after UCL and Middlesex University decided the former health and medical education campus was no longer fit for purpose.

Latest public status Mayor of London call-in

Islington rejected the 2024 application in March 2025. The Mayor called it in on 31 March 2025, so the GLA is now the decision maker.

Archway Campus moved from public healthcare to university medical use, then into a long redevelopment process. The central tension has stayed consistent: how to reuse a sensitive historic site while meeting housing, affordable housing, heritage, height, and public realm expectations.

Main points

From university exit to mayoral decision

  1. April 2012

    UCL and Middlesex start the exit process

    UCL announced an in-principle decision to sell the jointly owned campus. It said the site housed medical school activity, informatics, clinical research and health services research, but the buildings needed prohibitively expensive upgrades.

    UCL disposal notice
  2. October 2013

    The campus is vacated

    UCL said the Archway Campus was on schedule to be fully vacated by the end of October. Teams moved to the Whittington, Royal Free, Bloomsbury and other UCL locations.

    UCL decant update
  3. 2014

    Peabody takes the site on

    The site was sold to Peabody, a housing association. Local reporting notes that conservation status was also applied around this point, making heritage impact a core planning issue.

    Islington Tribune background
  4. 2019

    Peabody's proposal is refused

    Peabody brought forward redevelopment plans, but Islington Council refused them. Local coverage identifies affordable housing as the decisive issue, with the council's 50% target in the foreground.

    Local report on the refusal
  5. July 2021

    SevenCapital buys Archway Campus

    SevenCapital acquired the 3.65-acre site from Peabody and framed it as a residential-led regeneration project with affordable housing, possible student accommodation, and private or community amenity space.

    SevenCapital acquisition announcement
  6. 2023

    Consultation restarts under the new owner

    SevenCapital ran consultation events in February and June 2023. The emerging proposal shifted the debate toward the mix of restored heritage buildings, new homes, student housing, public space and height.

    Archway Campus consultation materials
  7. September 2024

    A new planning application is validated

    Application P2024/2598/FUL was validated by Islington on 24 September 2024. SevenCapital describes the scheme as residential-led, with 178 homes, 50% affordable housing, student housing, commercial uses, landscaping, pedestrian connections, and restoration of heritage assets.

    GLA application timeline Developer proposal summary
  8. March 2025

    Islington refuses; the Mayor calls it in

    Islington rejected the application in March 2025. On 31 March 2025, the Mayor of London called it in, moving the final decision from the borough to the GLA. The hearing date is still to be confirmed; as of 13 May 2026, a decision looks most likely in 2026 rather than years later, because City Hall lists Archway Campus as a future hearing and the Mayor usually decides at the hearing or within five working days if the decision is deferred.
